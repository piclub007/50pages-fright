The first pattern emerges in witness reports from different decades, different countries. Always the same details, never communicated between witnesses.
They speak of a presence that isn't seen, but felt in the stillness between moments. A quiet that has weight. A silence that listens.
The accounts describe a gradual awareness that develops over weeks or months. A sense of being observed during solitary activities: reading late at night, working alone, waking before dawn.
No physical manifestation. No voices. Just the growing certainty that something is attending. Waiting. Learning the patterns of a life.
The witnesses never report harm. Only observation. Only presence. Only the quiet.
"It doesn't feel malicious. Just... attentive. Like something learning how to be human by watching."
— Excerpt from interview #47, 1998
