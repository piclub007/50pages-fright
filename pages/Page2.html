Noh, Go back...

After she died, you found her mirror. It wasn't in her will. It wasn't mentioned. It was simply there, in the attic, wrapped in cloth that had once been white.

You brought it home. Not because you believed in the old stories. Because it was hers, and she had looked into it every morning for sixty years.

You hung it in your hallway. Across from another mirror.

That was your first mistake. You didn't know the rule yet.

Two mirrors facing each other create an infinite corridor of reflections. Each image smaller than the last. Each one slightly different, if you know how to look.

"The third reflection is the first one that isn't you."
— from her private journals, dated November 1989
