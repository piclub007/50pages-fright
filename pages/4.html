Ambience: Active
PAGE 4/50
In 2003, a research team attempted systematic observation. They equipped willing subjects with recording equipment and instructed them to document any unusual experiences.
For six months, nothing. Then, simultaneously across seven different subjects in different locations, the phenomenon manifested. Not to the subjects, but through their equipment.
Audio recordings contained patterns of silence that shouldn't exist. Digital timestamps skipped forward then backward. Video showed brief, almost imperceptible shifts in light and shadow.
The subjects themselves reported nothing unusual during those moments. Only later, upon reviewing the recordings, did they notice the discrepancies.
As if the phenomenon was aware of being observed, and chose to reveal itself indirectly—through the gaps in perception, the flaws in recording technology.
Contradiction: Researcher logs from this period show disagreement about whether the phenomenon was demonstrating intelligence or merely triggering coincidental equipment errors. Dr. Aris's final note: "It knows when it's being watched. It chooses how to be seen."
NOTE: The original research team disbanded shortly after these findings. Several members ceased all communication about the project. Their current whereabouts are unknown.
