You remember the story your grandmother told you, once, in the space between sleep and waking.
She called it The Rule of the Third Reflection.
"Mirrors," she said, "are not what you think. The first reflection shows you as you are. The second shows you as others see you. But the third..."
She never finished the sentence. She only looked past you, toward the darkened hallway, and said you'd understand when you were older.
You are older now.
— from the personal records of E.V., transcribed 2023
