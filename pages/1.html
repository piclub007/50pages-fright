The archive opens. A collection of pages, recovered from various sources over several years.
Some were found in abandoned research facilities. Others appeared in private collections, their origins unclear. A few were shared anonymously, always with the same instruction: "Continue the sequence."
Together, they form what we now call the 50 Pages of Fright. Not because they contain explicit horror, but because of the pattern that emerges when read in order. A slow dawning. A gradual understanding.
This is page one. The beginning of that pattern.
