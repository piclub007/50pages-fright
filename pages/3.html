Noh, Go back...

You began to notice it on the third night.

Not in the first reflection — that one behaved normally, copying your movements with dutiful precision. Not in the second either, slightly dimmer, slightly slower.

But the third. The one at the far end of the corridor of light.

It was standing when you stood. Turning when you turned. But there was a delay. A hesitation. As if it had to remember how to move.

What would you do if your reflection delayed by one second?
